PourBot
Smart Pourover Scale

PourBot is a DIY smart scale for manual coffee brewing. It combines a compact Waveshare ESP32-S3 Touch AMOLED, an HX711 load-cell amplifier, a precision load cell, battery power, on-screen brew controls, calibration tools, and a browser-based firmware installer.

What PourBot Does

Assistedbrewing

Large on-screen weight, timer, tare, start/pause, and reset controls designed for the small round AMOLED display.

Brew progress ring

The center display shows your current brew progress and helps you see how close you are to the target weight at a glance.

Calibration tools

Built-in scale calibration and settings pages make it easier to tune the load cell after assembly.

Battery Powered

Designed around the Waveshare board battery connector and power monitoring support for portable coffee brewing.

Web Dashboard

The firmware includes a browser-accessible dashboard and setup pages for settings, diagnostics, and recipes.

Easy Updates

Use the web installer below to flash new PourBot firmware directly from Chrome or Edge with a data-capable USB-C cable.

Step-by-Step Build Guide

Print and inspect the enclosure.Make sure the load-cell mount is flat, rigid, and free of warped surfaces. Any flex in the case can show up as drift or unstable readings.
Mount the fixed side of the load cell.Bolt the fixed end of the load cell firmly to the lower body of the enclosure. Do not overtighten to the point that the sensor body twists.
Mount the brew platform to the live side.The top plate should attach only to the sensing side of the load cell. Confirm it moves freely and does not rub the case.
Wire the load cell to the HX711.Connect the four load-cell leads to E+, E-, A+, and A- on the HX711. If readings move backwards, swap A+ and A-.
Connect the HX711 to the ESP32-S3.Connect VCC, GND, data, and clock according to the project hardware configuration. Keep signal wires neat and separated from battery leads when possible.
Install the ESP32-S3 AMOLED module.Seat the display/controller in the front opening, route wiring carefully, and confirm the touchscreen is not under mechanical stress.
Connect the battery.Use the Waveshare battery connector and verify polarity before plugging it in. Charge and test by USB-C before closing the enclosure.
Flash PourBot firmware.Use the installer below from Chrome or Edge. Select the ESP32-S3 USB serial port when prompted.
Calibrate the scale.Boot PourBot, tare with the platform empty, then run calibration with a known reference weight. Save the calibration value when readings look stable.
Final test with a real brew setup.Place your server and dripper on the scale, tare, start the brew timer, and confirm the touch buttons, weight reading, and progress ring behave as expected.
Safety note: LiPo batteries can be damaged by shorts, punctures, reversed polarity, or crushed wiring. Inspect the wiring before closing the case and never leave a damaged battery installed.

Firmware Installer

Flash the latest included PourBot firmware files directly from this page. The installer works best in Chrome or Edge on a desktop computer.

Ready to flash PourBot?
Plug the Waveshare ESP32-S3 into your computer with a data-capable USB-C cable, then click the button and choose the correct USB serial port.
Flashing may erase stored settings. After installing, check Wi-Fi settings, tare behavior, battery display, and scale calibration before your next brew.
